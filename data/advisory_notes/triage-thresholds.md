SYNTHETIC SAMPLE — not real clinical guidance.

Our clinical advisory board's recurring concern for a lung-nodule triage SaMD is
where the operating threshold sits. Radiologists want the malignancy-risk score
mapped explicitly onto Lung-RADS categories rather than presented as an opaque
0-100 number, because triage recommendations that don't reconcile with Lung-RADS
create workflow friction and medico-legal ambiguity. The board flagged that most
published validation reports characterise stand-alone AUC but under-report the
sensitivity/specificity actually achieved at the vendor's shipped default
threshold — which is the number that matters clinically. A gap analysis should
explicitly check whether the evidence base reports operating-point performance,
not just AUC.
