SYNTHETIC SAMPLE — not real clinical guidance.

The board stresses that "detection", "malignancy-risk estimation", and
"follow-up recommendation" are three different intended uses with escalating
regulatory and liability weight, and that products blur them in marketing. A
device cleared for detection/marking that surfaces a risk score users interpret
as a management recommendation is operating beyond its intended use. The gap
analysis should locate exactly where the target product and its predicates sit
on this spectrum, and flag any mismatch between what the predicate 510(k) was
cleared for and the capability the product intends to ship. The board also wants
explicit treatment of the failure mode where the tool is silent on a malignant
nodule (false negative) versus flags a benign one (false positive).
