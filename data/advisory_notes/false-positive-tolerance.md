SYNTHETIC SAMPLE — not real clinical guidance.

The board distinguishes sharply between a screening context and an incidental
context. In lung-cancer screening (low prevalence), a high false-positive rate
per scan drives downstream CT follow-ups, biopsies, and patient anxiety, and the
board considers false-positive burden the single largest adoption risk for a
detection SaMD. In the incidental-finding context the tolerance is different.
The board also raised automation bias: if the tool is framed as a "second
reader" vs a "concurrent reader" vs a "triage" device, the human-factors risk
profile changes, and the intended-use statement must be explicit about which.
Reader studies that measure the change in radiologist performance *with vs
without* the aid are considered essential evidence and are frequently missing.
